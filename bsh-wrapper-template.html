Canada Evictions Scoring Map

This interactive map provides a comprehensive comparison of eviction laws across Canadian provinces and territories. Using a rigorous scoring system, we evaluate 10 key indicators that measure tenant protections and procedural safeguards in eviction processes.

How to use this map:
Select an indicator from the right sidebar to view provincial scores. Click on any province or territory to see detailed information about that jurisdiction's eviction laws and protections.

Each province and territory is scored on a scale of 1 to 5 for each indicator, where 1 represents minimal protections and 5 represents comprehensive tenant safeguards. The map uses color coding from red (lower scores) to green (higher scores) for easy visual comparison.

About Our Methodology

Our scoring system evaluates eviction laws based on 10 critical indicators:

  • Notice of Termination: Required notice periods and termination procedures
  • Hearing & Decision: Tribunal/court processes and tenant participation rights
  • Order of Possession: Enforcement procedures and tenant safeguards
  • Rent Control: Rent increase limitations and protections
  • Above Guideline Increases: Processes for rent increases beyond standard limits
  • Eviction Types: Restrictions on grounds for eviction
  • Compensation: Financial protections and compensation requirements
  • Appeals: Availability and accessibility of appeal processes
  • Onus & Filing Fee: Burden of proof and cost barriers
  • Conditional Orders: Options for conditional eviction orders

About This Project

This research project systematically analyzes and compares residential eviction laws across Canada. By scoring each jurisdiction on key indicators, we provide policymakers, researchers, and advocates with data-driven insights into tenant protections and procedural fairness.

The project is part of ongoing research at BSH - UBC examining housing policy, tenant rights, and eviction prevention strategies across Canadian jurisdictions.
